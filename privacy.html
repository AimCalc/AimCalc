Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 12, 2026

AimCalc is a ballistic calculator for iPhone and Apple Watch. It is built to work entirely on your device. We do not operate any servers, and the app contains no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party SDKs.

What the app stores, and where

  • Gun and ammo profiles, targets, and settings are stored locally on your device, and mirrored to your paired Apple Watch using Apple's WatchConnectivity framework. This data never leaves your devices and is never sent to us — we have no way to see it.
  • Dope cards you save go to your own photo library, using add-only access (the app cannot read or browse your photos).
  • Range journal entries — including notes, target photos, and the solution and conditions snapshot — are stored in the app's private storage on your device only. Deleting an entry deletes its photo; deleting the app deletes the journal. If you choose a photo from your library, the system picker hands the app just that one image.
  • Your Terms of Use acceptance (agreement version and date) is recorded on your device only.

Location

If you grant location access, the app uses your position for two things only:

  • To request current weather conditions (temperature, pressure, humidity, wind) through Apple's WeatherKit service. Your coordinates are sent to Apple to answer that request, under Apple's privacy policy. We never receive or store them.
  • To compute the Coriolis correction, which happens entirely on your device.

If you create a range journal entry, the most recently captured coordinates are saved into that entry — on your device only, like the rest of the journal.

Location is optional. Every environmental value can be entered manually, and the app is fully functional with location denied.

Camera and motion sensors

The point-at-target incline feature shows a live camera view so you can put the crosshair on your target, and reads the device's motion sensors to measure the angle; that camera image is displayed only — never captured, stored, or transmitted. The camera can also take a target photo for a range journal entry, but only when you tap the shutter, and the photo goes only into the app's on-device journal storage. Nothing is transmitted anywhere.

Support email

If you contact support or suggest a feature, the app opens a pre-filled draft in your own mail app — nothing is sent unless you send it. If you do, we receive your email address and whatever you wrote, along with basic app diagnostics included in the draft (app version, iOS version, device model, active profile name, unit settings). We use this only to respond to you and do not share it with anyone.

Data we collect

None. We collect no personal data, usage data, identifiers, or diagnostics. There is nothing to sell, share, or breach on our end, because nothing is transmitted to us.

Children

AimCalc is a tool for lawful shooting sports and hunting, and is not directed at children.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted at this page with a new effective date. Because the app has no data collection to expand, changes are expected to be rare and editorial.
